Strategic Context

Five Meta-Narratives

Before the cluster-by-cluster breakdown, five overarching friction patterns emerge from reading the full pipeline.

1
Power User Ceiling

The platform is built for marketers, but data engineers keep showing up

Every mature client hits a moment where the UI isn't enough. They need SQL for calculated attributes, API access to programmatic exports, and operator logic that matches their data model. Clusters 1, 3, and 4 all trace back to this root: the abstraction layer is optimised for simplicity, but complex clients want to reach through it.

Clusters 1, 3, 4 Marktplaats · Kleinanzeigen · Edeka
2
Data Freshness

When was my data last updated, and can I control when it refreshes?

Audience refresh scheduling, ingestion monitoring, and alerts are all the same enterprise anxiety in different clothes. Clients have downstream SLAs and media campaigns that depend on knowing data is fresh. The platform confirms ingestion happened — but not with enough granularity for enterprise operations.

Clusters 2, 8 Edeka · Sky · REWE · Kleinanzeigen
3
Always-On Automation

Journeys are built, but they can't react to the customer's actual behaviour

Clients want journeys that trigger within minutes of an event, not batch runs. The gap between "scheduled journey" and "event-triggered journey" is the gap between batch marketing and real-time personalisation. With only 19% GA, Cluster 7 is the most under-delivered major theme in the pipeline.

Clusters 6, 7 Sky IT · O2 · Mediamarkt
4
Unmeasured Destination

Audiences are sent to destinations, but nobody can see what happened next

Cluster 3 (74 tickets) is the largest concrete cluster. The majority of requests are about visibility: match rates, send counts, export logs. Clients cannot optimise what they cannot measure. Many are exporting to spreadsheets as a workaround.

Cluster 3 74 tickets · 34% GA
5
RBAC Too Coarse

The permission model works for small teams — not for enterprise multi-market deployments

Every enterprise client with multi-team operations hits the RBAC ceiling. At 42% rejection, Cluster 9 has the highest rejection rate — yet demand keeps growing. This is now a pre-sales obstacle in financial services, retail media, and broadcasting procurement assessments.

Cluster 9 42% rejection rate
